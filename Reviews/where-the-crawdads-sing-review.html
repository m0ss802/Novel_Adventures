Where the Crawdads Sing by Delia Owens Book Review

3 stars

A beautiful and lyrical ode to the natural world of the Carolina marshes.

I loved Kya's gentle, though broken, nature. I felt for her as she was repeatedly abandoned, adn saw myself in her inward retreat, realizing she could trust only what she could depend on: herself and her marsh. Or at least, that's her worldview and learned belief set. Ultimately, I was unsure of the author's intended message. In some ways, it seems she is trying to subvert the noble savage theme by playing into the disgusting savage theme. And although the lyrical descriptions of the natural world were beautiful, otherwise the writing (and particularly the dialogue) were rather unimpressive.
